# Cardiovascular-Kidney-Metabolic (CKM) Health and HFpEF

> **Disclaimer:** This document is general health education, paraphrased from public
> patient resources. It is **not medical advice** and is **not specific to any individual**.

## What is cardiovascular-kidney-metabolic (CKM) syndrome?

Cardiovascular-kidney-metabolic (CKM) syndrome is a term used to describe the way **heart
health, kidney health, and metabolic conditions (such as obesity and type 2 diabetes) are
closely connected** and influence one another. Problems in one of these systems can lead to
or worsen problems in the others. For example, diabetes and high blood pressure can damage
the kidneys, and reduced kidney function can in turn put more strain on the heart.

## Why CKM matters for people with HFpEF

HFpEF very often occurs alongside metabolic and kidney conditions. Excess weight, type 2
diabetes, high blood pressure, and chronic kidney disease are common in people with HFpEF and
can make symptoms worse. Because these conditions are linked, **treating them together —
rather than one at a time in isolation — tends to give the best results.**

A practical example is the SGLT2 inhibitor class of medicines. These drugs were developed for
diabetes but have been shown to **benefit the heart and the kidneys as well**, which is why
they are useful across several of the conditions in CKM syndrome.

## What this means for everyday care

- Keeping **blood pressure, blood sugar, and cholesterol** in target ranges
- Maintaining a **healthy weight** and an active lifestyle as approved by your clinician
- Having **kidney function checked** regularly through blood and urine tests
- Coordinating care so that treatments for one condition support, rather than work against,
  the others

Managing CKM health is a long-term, team-based effort. Your clinician can help you understand
which numbers to watch and how your conditions affect each other.
