# Questions to Ask Your Doctor About HFpEF

> **Disclaimer:** This document is general health education, paraphrased from public
> patient resources. It is **not medical advice**. Use these questions as a starting point
> for a conversation with your own clinician.

## Understanding your condition

- What is my **ejection fraction**, and what does that number mean for me?
- What is causing or contributing to my HFpEF (for example, blood pressure, diabetes, or
  kidney disease)?
- How severe is my heart failure, and what should I expect over time?

## Questions about treatment options

- Which **medications** do you recommend for me, and what is each one meant to do?
- Would an **SGLT2 inhibitor** be appropriate for my situation?
- Do I need a **diuretic** to manage fluid, and how should I take it?
- What are the **possible side effects** of my medicines, and what should I watch for?
- How do my **kidney function and blood sugar** affect which treatments are safe for me?

## Questions about daily life and self-care

- How much **sodium (salt)** and **fluid** should I aim for each day?
- What kind of **physical activity** is safe and helpful for me?
- How often should I **weigh myself**, and what weight change should I report to you?
- How do I manage HFpEF along with my other conditions (such as diabetes or high blood
  pressure)?

## Questions about staying safe

- Which symptoms mean I should **call you**, and which mean I should **call 911**?
- Are there any **over-the-counter medicines or supplements** I should avoid?
- Who should I contact after office hours if my symptoms get worse?

Bringing a written list of questions, and a current list of your medicines, can help you get
the most out of each appointment.
